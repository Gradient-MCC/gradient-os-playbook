‚ö° Quick Win #2: Context Management

The AI only knows what you tell it. More context = better output. But there's a right way to do it.

üìã
Front-load context
Don't drip information. Give everything relevant upfront: background, constraints, examples of what good looks like, what to avoid. One rich prompt beats five back-and-forths.
üìé
Paste, don't describe
Instead of "I have a spreadsheet with sales data," paste the actual data. Instead of "based on our brand voice," paste your brand guidelines. Let the AI see what you see.
üîó
Build on conversations
Don't start fresh every time. Continue conversations when working on the same project. The AI remembers context within a session‚Äîuse it.
üéØ
Know when to reset
Long conversations can drift. If the AI seems confused or outputs degrade, start fresh with a clean, well-structured prompt. Sometimes a reset is faster than course-correction.

üîÑ The Iteration Mindset

Level 1 takes the first output. Level 2+ knows the magic is in the refinement.

1
Get the first draft

Use R.O.C.K. to get a solid starting point. Don't expect perfection.

2
Identify what's off

Too formal? Too long? Missing something? Be specific about what needs to change.

3
Refine with precision

"Make the intro punchier" or "Add a section about pricing" ‚Äî targeted feedback gets targeted improvements.

4
Know when to stop

80% from AI + 20% human polish often beats endless AI iteration. Add your voice at the end.
