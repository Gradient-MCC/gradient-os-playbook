‚ö° Quick Win #1: The R.O.C.K. Framework

You already know to give context. The difference is structured context vs. scattered context. R.O.C.K. makes sure nothing critical gets left out.

‚ùå Typical approach
Analyze our Q4 campaign performance and recommend where to increase budget next quarter. We ran paid social, search, and programmatic.

‚Üí What's your success metric? ROAS? CAC? Revenue?
‚Üí What were the actual numbers?
‚Üí Budget constraints? Business priorities?
‚Üí Who's the audience for this rec?

You'll get generic best practices, not your answer.

‚úÖ R.O.C.K. Applied
[R] You're a performance marketing strategist presenting to the CFO. [O] Recommend Q1 budget allocation across channels. Goal: maximize ROAS while maintaining brand reach. [C] Q4 results: [paste actual data]. Total budget: $150K. CFO prioritizes efficiency, but CEO wants market presence. [K] Format: 3 scenarios (conservative/balanced/aggressive). Include trade-offs, projected ROAS, risk factors. One-pager for exec review.

üéØ R.O.C.K. Breakdown

Each letter answers a question the AI will guess at (poorly) if you don't provide it.

R ‚Äî Role
Who's writing this? For whom?
"You're a performance marketing strategist presenting to the CFO"

This changes everything‚Äîvocabulary, level of detail, what to emphasize. A strategist for CFO focuses on ROI; same content for CEO emphasizes market position.
O ‚Äî Objective
What decision does this enable?
"Goal: maximize ROAS while maintaining brand reach"

Not "analyze data" but "recommend budget allocation." The tension between two goals (efficiency vs. reach) shapes the entire analysis.
C ‚Äî Context
What does the AI need to see?
"Q4 results: [paste actual data]. CFO prioritizes efficiency, CEO wants market presence."

The real numbers. The internal politics. The constraints nobody thinks to mention but everyone knows.
K ‚Äî Keys
What's the deliverable format?
"3 scenarios. Include trade-offs, projected ROAS, risk factors. One-pager."

Your acceptance criteria upfront. Without these, you'll be back saying "can you add a conservative option?" "make it shorter"

üìã The Context Rule: Paste, Don't Describe

You know to "give context." But there's a difference between describing context and showing it.

‚ùå Describing (you're still translating)
The campaign performance was mixed. Paid social did okay, search was strong, programmatic underperformed. We spent about $150K total.

What's "mixed"? What's "okay"? You've already interpreted the data‚Äîthe AI can't challenge or find patterns you missed.

‚úÖ Pasting (let AI see raw truth)
Here's our Q4 performance export: Channel | Spend | Revenue | ROAS Paid Social | $52K | $89K | 1.71 Search | $68K | $204K | 3.00 Programmatic | $30K | $36K | 1.20

Now AI can do math, spot ratios, find the real story‚Äînot your interpretation of it.

üìã What to paste: data exports, client emails, briefs, competitor pages, style samples, feedback threads

Anything you're summarizing, paste instead. Let AI do the summarizing‚Äîthat's what it's good at.

üîÑ Quick Win #1 in Action

R.O.C.K. + Paste Context + Iterate. Here's the complete pattern.

1
Set up with R.O.C.K.

[R] Who's writing, for whom ‚Üí [O] What decision this enables ‚Üí [K] Deliverable format, constraints

2
Paste, don't describe

[C] Raw data, actual emails, real briefs‚Äîwhatever context exists in your world, let AI see it directly.

3
First output is a draft

Expect to iterate. "Emphasize X more" ‚Üí "Cut the second section" ‚Üí "Add specific numbers from the data" ‚Üí Each round gets closer.

4
Human polish at 80%

Stop when you're 80% there. The last 20% is your expertise, judgment, and the things only you know. That's the human value.

‚ö° This alone will 2-3x your output quality

Most people never get past step 1. Just using this pattern puts you ahead of 90% of AI users.

üìù Creating Your First Skill

In Claude Desktop, Skills are just saved prompts with a trigger. Here's how to make one.

1
Write your best prompt

Use R.O.C.K. to structure it. Include your Role, Objective, default Context, and Keys. Mark where variable info goes with [brackets].

2
Save it as a Skill

In Claude Desktop: Settings ‚Üí Skills ‚Üí Create Skill. Give it a name (this becomes your /command) and paste your prompt template.

3
Use it

Type /your-skill-name in any conversation. Your template loads. Fill in the [brackets] with this-time-specific context. Done.
